About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Department of Resource Economics at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. In Fall 2025, I will join the O’Neill School of Public and Environmental Affairs (SPEA) at Indiana University Bloomington as an Assistant Professor (tenure-track).

As an environmental and natural resource economist, my research examines the interactions between human and environmental systems, with a focus on health, land use, and water resources.

My work pursues two key objectives:

  • Quantifying the economic and health impacts of land and water policies.
  • Developing Integrated Assessment Models (IAMs) to address water quality challenges, particularly nutrient pollution.

I hold a Ph.D. in Natural Resource Economics and Environmental Informatics from Virginia Tech, USA. Additionally, I have degrees in Development Economics from Brazil and Mechanical Engineering from India.
